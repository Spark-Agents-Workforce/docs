spark is an AI-powered intelligence platform for teams.

you plug in your tools. two data streams start flowing. you choose which AI agents run on top of those streams. and you get an AI you can talk to that sees everything.

that's the whole product.

## two streams

everything in spark starts with data flowing in.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="the activity stream" icon="activity" href="/streams/activity">
    connect your tools. events flow in automatically. they naturally group into **sessions** — scored units of work. "kevin worked on the checkout refactor for 2h15m across 14 commits."
  </Card>
  <Card title="the knowledge stream" icon="book-open" href="/streams/knowledge">
    drop in what you know. research papers, tutorials, competitive intel, meeting notes. each one is a **knowledge unit** that the system reads, extracts, and synthesizes into a living wiki.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

two streams. one is automatic (your tools generate it). one is intentional (you feed it). both compound over time.

## agents

agents are AI watchers that process your streams. they're the intelligence layer.

every agent is built from three capabilities:

<Steps>
  <Step title="watch" icon="radio">
    monitor a stream for specific events or patterns.
  </Step>
  <Step title="evaluate" icon="scan">
    AI decides what it means, scores confidence.
  </Step>
  <Step title="act" icon="zap">
    take an action — score it, alert someone, create a page, route a message.
  </Step>
</Steps>

some agents use all three. some just watch and evaluate. all are toggleable. turn on what matters, turn off what's noise.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="on by default">
    - **impact scorer** — watches sessions, evaluates impact across output/importance/quality, assigns scores
    - **synthesis generator** — watches session accumulation, generates daily and weekly narrative summaries
    - **signal puller** — watches external tools for non-code events (tasks, messages, tickets)
    - **knowledge ingest** — watches for new knowledge sources, extracts entities, synthesizes wiki pages
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="available to enable">
    - PR quality monitor, slack urgency detector, deploy watcher, shopify health monitor, knowledge lint, and more
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## spark

spark is the AI you talk to. it sits on top of everything — both streams, everything the agents produce. ask anything.

"how's the team doing this week?" · "what do we know about react performance?" · "compare Q1 output to Q2." · "what shipped yesterday?"

answers come back as rich widgets. charts, tables, leaderboards, timelines. conversations are persistent threads. insights can be saved back into the knowledge stream where they compound with everything else.

## who spark is for

teams that work with AI agents, automated pipelines, and complex toolchains. engineering teams. devops. AI-native startups. any team where work moves faster than humans can track.
