the knowledge stream is the second input to spark. while the activity stream captures what your team **does**, the knowledge stream captures what your team **knows**.

drop in what you learn. a research paper. a tutorial you wrote. a competitor's blog post. meeting notes. a thread that changed your thinking. each one enters the system as a **knowledge unit** and gets processed by the knowledge ingest agent into a living, cross-referenced wiki.

## why this exists

most teams' knowledge is scattered. docs rot in notion. meeting notes disappear into slack. research lives in someone's head. competitive intel sits in a google doc nobody updates.

the problem isn't that the knowledge doesn't exist. it's that nobody maintains it.

<Note>
  AI does. it doesn't get bored. it doesn't forget to update a cross-reference. it can touch 15 files in one pass. the maintenance cost is near zero.
</Note>

## how it compounds

<Steps>
  <Step title="week 1" icon="calendar">
    20 pages with a few cross-references. useful but sparse.
  </Step>
  <Step title="month 3" icon="trending-up">
    200 pages with hundreds of connections. patterns emerge that no single source contained.
  </Step>
  <Step title="month 6" icon="brain">
    an organizational brain that knows more about your domain than any individual on the team.
  </Step>
</Steps>
