# the knowledge stream

> the second input — what your team knows, compounding over time.

the activity stream captures what your team **does**. the knowledge stream captures what your team **knows**.

drop in what you learn. a research paper. a tutorial you wrote. a competitor's blog post. meeting notes. a thread that changed your thinking. each one enters the system as a **knowledge unit** and gets processed into a living, cross-referenced wiki.

the knowledge stream compounds. week one, you have 20 pages with a few cross-references. by month three, you have 200 pages with hundreds of connections — patterns that no single source contained. by month six, you have an organizational brain that knows more about your domain than any individual on the team.

## the bridge between streams

the activity stream and knowledge stream aren't silos. they connect.

- sessions can become knowledge sources — when work produces insights worth preserving, push the session into the knowledge stream
- knowledge informs sessions — when you view work, spark shows related knowledge
- spark AI draws on both — ask a question and it queries activity data and knowledge pages together to give you the full picture

two streams. connected. feeding the same AI.
