# the activity stream

> how events flow in from your tools and naturally form into sessions.

the activity stream is the foundation. everything in spark starts here.

you connect your tools. events flow in. the system watches the stream and groups related events into **sessions** — scored, AI-analyzed units of work. raw tool noise in, meaningful intelligence out.

## events

every meaningful action across your team's tools becomes an event. a commit. a PR merged. a deploy. a slack thread. an asana task moved to done. a shopify order.

each event carries four things:

| field         | what it is                                                                                   |
| ------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **actor**     | who did it — a person, an AI agent, a bot                                                    |
| **source**    | where it came from — github, slack, asana, linear, shopify, or a custom webhook              |
| **timestamp** | when it happened — millisecond precision, used for session grouping and timeline construction |
| **payload**   | what happened — the commit message, the PR title, the task name, the order total              |

events are immutable. once they're in the stream, they don't change. they're the raw record.

<Tip>events arrive in real-time. no batch imports, no polling, no nightly syncs. when someone pushes a commit, it's in the stream within seconds.</Tip>

## the feed

the activity feed is your view into the stream. a live, chronological timeline of everything happening across your team.

you can view it at two levels of resolution:

- **event level** — every individual action, in order. granular. useful when you need to trace exactly what happened and when.
- **session level** — grouped, scored work units. this is where most people live. you see what people accomplished, not the individual keystrokes that got them there.

filter by actor, source, repo, time range, or event type. the feed updates in real-time — when someone's working, you can watch their session grow.

## what happens next

events on their own are just a log. the real value comes when the system groups them into [sessions](/streams/sessions) — scored, AI-analyzed units of work that tell you what happened, why it mattered, and how it connects to everything else.
