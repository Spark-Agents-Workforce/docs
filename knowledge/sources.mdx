sources are the raw inputs to your knowledge stream. every piece of knowledge enters the system as a source. an immutable document that the ai reads, extracts from, and synthesizes into wiki pages.

## types of sources

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="url" icon="link">
    paste a link. spark fetches the page, converts it to readable text, and processes it. articles, blog posts, documentation, github readmes, research papers with public urls.
  </Card>
  <Card title="document" icon="file-text">
    upload a file. pdfs, markdown files, text documents. drag and drop or use the upload button. supports files up to 25mb.
  </Card>
  <Card title="note" icon="pen-line">
    type or paste raw text. your observations, ideas, quick notes from a conversation. dont worry about formatting. the ai structures it.
  </Card>
  <Card title="session" icon="layers">
    any spark session can become a source. when a work session produces insights, decisions, or patterns worth remembering, push it to knowledge. the ai extracts what matters and integrates it into the wiki.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## shooting it in

the fastest path from "i learned something" to "its in the system" is the quick-add modal. accessible from anywhere in spark. the sidebar always has an "add knowledge" button.

<Note>three tabs: paste a url, upload a file, or type a note. one action. done.</Note>

this should feel like tweeting for your teams brain. low friction, high value. you dont think about where it goes or how it gets organized. you just push it in.

## what happens after upload

when a source arrives, the knowledge ingest agent runs a multi-step pipeline:

<Steps>
  <Step title="chunk" icon="scissors">
    large documents get split into manageable segments
  </Step>
  <Step title="extract" icon="scan">
    ai reads the source and identifies entities (people, tools, concepts, projects), claims (assertions and facts), and relationships
  </Step>
  <Step title="synthesize" icon="combine">
    creates new wiki pages or updates existing ones, integrating new information into whats already known
  </Step>
  <Step title="cross-reference" icon="git-compare">
    scans for connections to other pages, creates typed links
  </Step>
  <Step title="attribute" icon="bookmark">
    links every claim back to its source, so you can always trace "where did this come from?"
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>one source can touch 10-15 wiki pages. you can watch the progress in real-time. the ingest tracker shows each step as it happens.</Tip>

## source management

the sources page shows everything youve fed the system:

| field | detail |
|-------|--------|
| status | pending, processing, processed, failed |
| type | url, document, note, session |
| when it was added | when it was added |
| what pages it contributed to | what pages it contributed to |

sources are immutable. the ai reads from them but never changes them. theyre your source of truth. the wiki is the ais interpretation. the sources are the evidence.
