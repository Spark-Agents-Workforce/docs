everything up to this point is about organizing what you already have. enrichment is about getting what you dont.

the ai doesnt just synthesize your sources. it identifies whats missing from your knowledge base and goes and finds it. searching the live web, not relying on training data from months ago.

this is the difference between a knowledge base and an intelligence platform.

## how it works

the ai analyzes your knowledge graph and identifies gaps:

<Note>
  "you have extensive pages about react but nothing about react server components, which three sources reference."

  "your competitor analysis page was last updated 3 months ago. the competitor has since launched two new features."

  "your api integration page references rate limiting but you have no documentation on retry patterns."
</Note>

then it acts:

<Steps>
  <Step title="searches the live web" icon="globe">
    current articles, documentation, research, discussions. todays data, not 6-month-old training data.
  </Step>
  <Step title="reads and evaluates" icon="scan">
    what it finds. is this relevant? is it reliable? does it fill the gap?
  </Step>
  <Step title="synthesizes" icon="combine">
    findings into your wiki with full source attribution. you know exactly where every enriched claim came from.
  </Step>
  <Step title="cross-references" icon="git-compare">
    against existing pages. does this new information confirm, extend, or contradict what you already know?
  </Step>
</Steps>

## what it looks for

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="gap-filling" icon="search">
    concepts your team references but hasnt documented. the ai creates the missing pages from authoritative external sources.
  </Card>
  <Card title="freshness checking" icon="refresh-cw">
    have things changed since a page was last updated? pricing changes, api updates, new versions, deprecated features. the ai checks whether external reality still matches your wiki.
  </Card>
  <Card title="competitive intelligence" icon="globe">
    when you have pages about competitors, the ai monitors for changes. new product launches, pricing shifts, feature announcements, public discussions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="related research" icon="book-open">
    when your wiki covers technical topics, the ai finds related papers, blog posts, and documentation that could deepen your understanding.
  </Card>
  <Card title="industry signals" icon="activity">
    emerging patterns, new tools, shifting best practices in your domain. not everything. just whats relevant to topics your wiki already covers.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## trust and attribution

enriched content is clearly marked. you always know:

- which claims came from your teams sources vs external enrichment
- the exact url and date of every external source
- when the enrichment happened
- whether the external information confirms or contradicts existing knowledge

youre never surprised by phantom information. every enriched claim is traceable, just like every ingested claim.

## the loop

this creates a powerful loop:

<Steps>
  <Step title="1">
    you push knowledge in (sources)
  </Step>
  <Step title="2">
    the ai synthesizes it (pages)
  </Step>
  <Step title="3">
    the ai identifies whats missing (gaps)
  </Step>
  <Step title="4">
    the ai goes and finds it (enrichment)
  </Step>
  <Step title="5">
    new enriched knowledge creates new connections
  </Step>
  <Step title="6">
    new connections reveal new gaps
  </Step>
  <Step title="7">
    back to step 4
  </Step>
</Steps>

your knowledge base gets smarter whether youre actively feeding it or not. the enrichment loop runs in the background, continuously filling gaps and checking freshness.

this is what makes spark a living brain, not a static wiki. the system doesnt just remember what you told it. it actively seeks out what you need to know.
