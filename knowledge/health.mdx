a knowledge base that nobody maintains eventually lies to you. pages go stale. contradictions creep in. important concepts get mentioned but never explained. sources get outdated.

sparks knowledge health system prevents this. the knowledge lint agent monitors your wiki and surfaces problems before they erode trust.

## what it checks

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="contradictions" icon="octagon-alert">
    two pages that disagree. "page a says we use postgres. page b says we migrated to convex." the ai identifies conflicting claims across pages and flags them with the specific excerpts that conflict.
  </Card>
  <Card title="stale pages" icon="clock-3">
    pages that havent been updated despite newer sources arriving on the same topic. if you ingested a competitor analysis in january and havent updated it since, but three newer sources mention that competitor, the page is stale.
  </Card>
  <Card title="orphans" icon="unlink">
    pages with no inbound links. nobody references them. are they still relevant, or have they drifted away from the rest of the knowledge base?
  </Card>
  <Card title="gaps" icon="search-x">
    concepts referenced across multiple pages but lacking their own dedicated page. "react server components is mentioned in 5 pages but doesnt have its own page." the ai can auto-create missing pages or flag them for human review.
  </Card>
  <Card title="low confidence" icon="triangle-alert">
    pages built from a single source. they might be correct, but they havent been corroborated. more sources covering the same topic increases confidence.
  </Card>
  <Card title="unsourced claims" icon="triangle-alert">
    assertions on a page that cant be traced back to any source. how did this get here? maybe it was manually added, maybe its a synthesis artifact. either way, it needs attribution.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## the knowledge lint agent

the lint agent is itself a watch + evaluate pattern:

<Steps>
  <Step title="watch" icon="radio">
    monitors the state of the knowledge base: page ages, reference patterns, source coverage, claim attributions
  </Step>
  <Step title="evaluate" icon="scan">
    identifies health issues, classifies severity, suggests fixes
  </Step>
</Steps>

it runs periodically. when it finds issues, they surface on the health dashboard with severity ratings.

for many issues, the ai can auto-resolve:

- stale pages can be refreshed from newer sources
- missing pages can be auto-created from existing references
- low-confidence pages can be flagged with a note explaining the limitation

contradictions and unsourced claims usually need human review. the ai flags them and shows the conflicting evidence, but a person decides which version is correct.

## the health dashboard

the health page shows:

| section | detail |
|---------|--------|
| **issue count by type** | how many contradictions, stale pages, orphans, gaps |
| **severity distribution** | critical, warning, info |
| **trending** | is health improving or degrading over time? |
| **individual issues** | each one with the affected pages, the evidence, and a suggested resolution |
| **resolved history** | what was fixed and when |

<Tip>enable the knowledge lint agent when your knowledge base has enough pages that maintenance matters. typically after 50+ pages. below that, manual review is enough.</Tip>
