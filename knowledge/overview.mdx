the knowledge stream is the second input to spark. while the activity stream captures what your team does, the knowledge stream captures what your team knows.

drop in what you learn. a research paper. a tutorial you wrote. a competitors blog post. meeting notes. a thread that changed your thinking. each one enters the system as a **knowledge unit** and gets processed by the knowledge ingest agent into a living, cross-referenced wiki.

## why this exists

most teams knowledge is scattered. docs rot in notion. meeting notes disappear into slack. research lives in someones head. competitive intel sits in a google doc nobody updates.

the problem isnt that the knowledge doesnt exist. its that nobody maintains it. humans abandon wikis because the maintenance burden grows faster than the value. updating cross-references, keeping summaries current, noting when new data contradicts old claims. nobody wants to do that.

<Note>
  ai does. it doesnt get bored. it doesnt forget to update a cross-reference. it can touch 15 files in one pass. the maintenance cost is near zero.
</Note>

## how it compounds

<Steps>
  <Step title="week 1" icon="calendar-days">
    you have 20 pages with a few cross-references. useful but sparse.
  </Step>
  <Step title="month 3" icon="network">
    200 pages with hundreds of connections. patterns emerge that no single source contained. contradictions have been flagged. concepts that seemed unrelated are now linked.
  </Step>
  <Step title="month 6" icon="brain">
    an organizational brain that knows more about your domain than any individual on the team. the knowledge base has been enriched with intelligence the ai found on its own. its not just what you put in. its what the system discovered.
  </Step>
</Steps>

this compounding effect is what makes knowledge different from a folder of files or a chatbot with memory. it genuinely gets smarter over time.

## the bridge

the activity stream and knowledge stream arent separate silos. they connect.

sessions can become knowledge sources. when work produces insights worth remembering, push it to knowledge. knowledge informs sessions. when you view work, spark shows related knowledge. and when you ask spark a question, it draws on both streams to give you the full picture.

<Note>two streams. connected. both feeding the same ai.</Note>
