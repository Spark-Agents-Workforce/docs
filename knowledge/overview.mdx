# knowledge

> a compounding knowledge base that gets smarter every day.

most teams' knowledge is scattered. docs rot in notion. meeting notes disappear into slack. research lives in someone's head. competitive intel sits in a google doc nobody updates.

the problem isn't that the knowledge doesn't exist. it's that nobody maintains it. humans abandon wikis because the maintenance burden grows faster than the value — updating cross-references, keeping summaries current, noting when new data contradicts old claims.

<Note>
  AI does. it doesn't get bored. it doesn't forget to update a cross-reference. it can touch 15 pages in one pass. the maintenance cost is near zero.
</Note>

## how it works

you feed knowledge in. the AI builds a living wiki from it.

<Steps>
  <Step title="feed" icon="file-plus">
    [push in sources](/knowledge/sources) — URLs, documents, notes, or sessions. each one is a knowledge unit.
  </Step>

  <Step title="synthesize" icon="combine">
    the knowledge ingest agent reads each source, extracts entities, concepts, and claims, and [writes wiki pages](/knowledge/pages) that integrate with everything already known.
  </Step>

  <Step title="connect" icon="git-compare">
    every page gets [linked to related pages](/knowledge/graph) with typed relationships — mentions, dependencies, contradictions, hierarchies. backlinks are automatic.
  </Step>

  <Step title="maintain" icon="shield-check">
    the [health system](/knowledge/health) monitors for contradictions, stale pages, orphans, gaps, and unsourced claims. issues get flagged or auto-resolved.
  </Step>

  <Step title="enrich" icon="search">
    the AI identifies what's missing and [goes to find it](/knowledge/enrichment) — searching the live web for current information, filling gaps, checking freshness.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## how it compounds

<Steps>
  <Step title="week 1" icon="calendar-days">
    20 pages with a few cross-references. useful but sparse.
  </Step>

  <Step title="month 3" icon="network">
    200 pages with hundreds of connections. patterns emerge that no single source contained. contradictions have been flagged. concepts that seemed unrelated are now linked.
  </Step>

  <Step title="month 6" icon="brain">
    an organizational brain. the system has been enriched with intelligence the AI found on its own. it's not just what you put in — it's what the system discovered.
  </Step>
</Steps>

this compounding effect is what makes knowledge different from a folder of files or a chatbot with memory. it genuinely gets smarter over time.
