spark is the AI you talk to. it sits on top of everything. your activities, sessions, scores, knowledge base. ask anything in natural language.

no dashboards to configure. no queries to write. no filters to fiddle with. just ask.

## ask anything

spark understands your team's data because it has direct access to every table in your observatory. it can read sessions, events, scores, signals, goals, team members, and your knowledge base.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="team overview">
    - "how's the team doing this week?"
    - "who's been the most productive this month?"
    - "what shipped yesterday?"
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="individual deep dives">
    - "show me everything kevin worked on in the last 7 days."
    - "what's dominic's average impact score this quarter vs last?"
    - "has anyone been working on the auth migration?"
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="strategic questions">
    - "compare frontend vs backend output this month."
    - "what work has the highest impact but lowest completion rate?"
    - "which repos have the most activity but no sessions scored above 100?"
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="cross-referencing">
    - "are there any sessions related to what our knowledge base says about react performance?"
    - "show me all deploys that happened within an hour of a high-urgency slack message."
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>spark doesn't just return data. it interprets it. it tells you what the numbers mean, what patterns it sees, and what you might want to pay attention to.</Tip>

## threads

conversations with spark are persistent threads. you can:

- **continue a conversation** — come back to a thread and pick up where you left off
- **build iteratively** — "show me this week's sessions." → "now filter to just kevin." → "compare that to last week." → "make that a bar chart."
- **save insights** — pin important findings so your team can see them
- **file to knowledge** — when spark surfaces something worth remembering, push the answer into your knowledge base where it compounds with everything else

<Note>threads aren't throwaway chat. they're working sessions where analysis happens. the conversation IS the analytical tool.</Note>

## tools

under the hood, spark has a toolkit of specialized operations:

| tool | what it does |
|------|-------------|
| **search knowledge** | semantic search across your wiki pages |
| **get page** | read any knowledge page in full |
| **add knowledge** | file new insights into the wiki |
| **query tables** | run structured queries across any observatory table |
| **time series** | generate trend data over custom periods |
| **live sessions** | check what's happening right now |

when you ask a complex question, spark chains multiple tools together. searching knowledge, querying sessions, comparing time periods. then synthesizes the results into a coherent answer.
